 Zimbabwe: Arbitrary arrests/Fear for Safety/Conditions of 				Detention/Prisoners of conscience, An estimated 1400 people. | Amnesty International 
 75/02 
 Around 1,400 people have been arrested during Zimbabwe's  presidential election, and are detained without charge in dangerous and overcrowded conditions that could be        life-threatening. Those in custody are opposition party   polling agents (who monitor vote-counting), independent   election observers and Zimbabwean citizens perceived to   be supporters of the main opposition party, the Movement  for Democratic Change (MDC).
