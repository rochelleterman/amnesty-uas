 Saudi Arabia: Incommunicado detention/Fear of torture or ill-treatment/ possible prisoner of conscience: Mohamed Saleh Osman al-Bajadi (m)

 | Amnesty International 
 Mohamed Saleh Osman al-Bajadi was arrested on 4 September in Buraida in the al-Qassim region, north of the capital, Riyadh. He is held incommunicado and is at risk of torture or other ill-treatment. Amnesty International believes that he may have been detained solely for the exercise of his conscientiously held beliefs and as such may be a prisoner of conscience.
