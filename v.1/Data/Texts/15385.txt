"Further information on EXTRA 75/94 (EUR 44/151/94, 9 December) - Turkey: possible ""disappearance"" / fear for safety: Faik Candan 
 Further information on EXTRA 75/94 (EUR 44/151/94, 9 December) - Turkey: possible ""disappearance"" / fear for safety: Faik Candan | Amnesty International 
 NA 
 The body of Kurdish lawyer Faik Candan was found on the morning of 14 December 1994, in a deserted area in the Salih Bey region, 15 km from a military base. He had been shot dead, presumably on 11 or 12 December. Faik Candan was aged 30 and was a father of two. He had been known as an outspoken critic of government policy towards the Kurdish minority and was president of the Ankara branch of the People's Labour Party (HEP) in 1992. He ""disappeared"" on 2 December."
